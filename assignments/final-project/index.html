Salt Lake Temple

Located in downtown Salt Lake City, Utah, the Salt Lake Temple is the most iconic LDS temple in the world. This magnificent granite structure was constructed over a period of 40 years, from the laying of the cornerstones in 1853 to the dedication on April 6th, 1893.

The location of the temple, called Temple Square, is the most-visited site in the state, drawing 3 - 5 million people each year. It was announced in 2019 that the temple will be closed for renovations on December 29, 2019 for four years.
